Facts about our ocean

The Ocean

The ocean covers more than 70% of the Earth Surface, making it the largest ecosystem in the planet

The Largest Ocean

The Pacific Ocean is the largest ocean in the world, covering an area of about 63.8 million square miles (165.2 million square kilometers).

What's in the Ocean

The ocean is home to more than one million known species of plants and animals, with many more still undiscovered

The Air We Breathe

The ocean is responsible for generating about 50% of the oxygen we breathe, thanks to tiny plants called phytoplankton that photosynthesize near the surface.

Absorb Carbon Dioxide

The ocean is also responsible for absorbing about 30% of the carbon dioxide we produce, which helps regulate the Earth's climate.

The Deepest Point of the Ocean

The deepest part of the ocean is the Mariana Trench, which is located in the western Pacific Ocean and reaches a depth of almost 36,070 feet (10,994 meters).

Economy and Transportation

The ocean is vital to global trade, with more than 90% of international trade carried by ships.

Recreation

The ocean also plays an important role in recreation, with activities like surfing, fishing, and boating attracting millions of people every year.

Features of Ocean

The ocean is divided into five major basins: the Atlantic, Pacific, Indian, Southern, and Arctic oceans, with average depth of around 12.080 feet (3,682 meters) and temperatures depending on its geographical location and depth, which on average is around 39°F (3.8°C). Water temperatures are warmer near the equator and cooler near the poles, and ocean water is salty, with an average salinity of about 35 parts per thousand. However, salinity levels can vary depending on factors like rainfall, evaporation, and freshwater inputs from rivers and streams.

Marine Biodiversity

Marine Biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms found below the ocean, including all plants, animals, and microorganisms that live in the marine environment. The ocean is considered to be one of the most biodiverse places on earth, with estimated more than 230.000 species currency identified, and is still counting.

Fish

One of the most easily recognized and well known living forms on the ocean, with the estimation of 33.000 species of fish, ranging from tiny seahorses to enormous whale sharks. Fish plays an important role in the marine ecosystem, as they are key food sources for many other species, and they also help regulate populations of other marine animals by consuming them.

Corals

Tiny, stationary animals that are built from accumulation of calcium carbonate skeletons produced by coral polyps with complex structures, forming the basis for reef ecosystems. Plays the role of providing critical habitat for a wide range of marine species.

Other Groups of Marine Animals

Other groups of marine animals, for instance marine mammals, whales, dolphins, seals; as well as wide range invertebrates like jellyfish, crabs, or squid; and marine microorganisms, phytoplankton and zooplankton also play an important role in marine ecosystems, forming the base of the ocean food chain and producing much of the oxygen we breathe.

Challenges of the Ocean

The ocean also plays the role of maintaining the global climate system and as temperatures continue to rise due to human-caused climate change, sea levels are also rising. This is due in part to the fact that warmer water expands, but also because melting ice from land-based glaciers and ice sheets is flowing into the ocean.

Ocean currents play a critical role in regulating the Earth's climate by moving heat from the equator towards the poles. However, as the planet warms, these currents are also shifting, potentially leading to major changes in weather patterns and ocean ecosystems.

The ocean is a major sink for carbon dioxide, which is leading to ocean acidification and increased sea surface temperatures. This has significant impacts on marine ecosystems, including coral reefs, fisheries, and biodiversity.

Many marine fish stocks are overexploited or depleted due to unsustainable fishing practices, such as bottom trawling, bycatch, and illegal, unreported, and unregulated fishing.

The ocean is polluted with plastic debris, oil spills, and other forms of pollution that harm marine life, disrupt food webs, and impact human health.

Human activities such as coastal development, dredging, and mining can destroy critical marine habitats, such as coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangroves.

Non-native species can be introduced into the ocean through shipping, aquaculture, and other activities, which can have significant ecological and economic impacts.

Solutions / Campaigns

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch Campaign
The campaign aims to raise awareness of the plastic pollution problem in the ocean and its impact on marine life.
Sea Shepherd Conservation Society Campaigns
Sea Shepherd is an international non-profit organization that aims to protect marine wildlife and their habitats. Their campaigns include patrolling and defending marine protected areas, opposing illegal fishing practices, and preventing the killing of whales and dolphins.
Greenpeace Save the Arctic Campaign
Greenpeace's campaign aims to protect the Arctic region from oil drilling and other destructive activities that could harm the fragile ecosystem and the livelihoods of local communities.
Mission Blue Campaign
Mission Blue is an initiative started by renowned oceanographer Dr. Sylvia Earle, which aims to create a global network of marine protected areas to protect and restore the health of the ocean.
Surfrider Foundation's Rise Above Plastics Campaign
The Surfrider Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to protecting the ocean and coasts from pollution and environmental harm. Their Rise Above Plastics campaign focuses on reducing plastic pollution in the ocean.
5 Gyres Campaign
The 5 Gyres campaign aims to raise awareness about the impact of plastic pollution on the world's oceans and to promote solutions to reduce and eliminate plastic waste.
Ocean Conservancy's Trash Free Seas Campaign
The Ocean Conservancy is a non-profit organization that works to protect the ocean from pollution and other threats. Their Trash Free Seas campaign focuses on reducing the amount of plastic waste that enters the ocean.
Save Our Seas Foundation Campaign
The Save Our Seas Foundation is a non-profit organization that works to protect the ocean and its marine life. Their campaigns focus on reducing plastic pollution, promoting sustainable fishing practices, and protecting endangered species.
Oceana's Save the Oceans, Feed the World Campaign
Oceana is an international organization dedicated to protecting and restoring the world's oceans. Their Save the Oceans, Feed the World campaign focuses on promoting sustainable fishing practices to protect marine life and ensure food security for communities that rely on seafood.
